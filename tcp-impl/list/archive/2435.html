    Is anybody aware of any study/paper about the performance of TCP over
    reliable media? I am interested in understanding how much of TCP performance
    is related to the fact that the network looses packets during congestions.
    In other words, if a point-to-point flow control (e.g. IEEE 802.3x) is
    implemented on every hop in the network, would the overall TCP throughput be
    better, worse or unchanged? I am also assuming that the network is made of
    switches/routers, which using 802.3x do not loose any packets.
