Hello,

        I am a graduate student and I am attempting to understand
the developmental direction of the applications which use
TCP/IP protocols to communicate through the network.

        I have seen in the discussion of NFSv4 an encouragement to
use TCP rather than UDP for communications on the network
due to the lack of congestion control in UDP.

        I have seen some discussions of changing UDP to have
congestion control mechanisms.

        I just read this morning Sally Floyd's ietf draft regarding
the use of ECN field within IP to pass information regarding
congestion to the encapsulating protocols.

        Would anyone be able to give me a good feel for what
direction TCP/IP will take with respect to congestion
control?

Paula DuFour
Graduate Student
Telecommunications and Computer
SEAS George Washington University
