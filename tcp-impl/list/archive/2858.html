    On Fri, 20 Sep 2002, Prakash Khemani wrote:

    > > The second call to setsockopt doesnot have any effect on the
    > > receive buffer.
    > > Does the TCP protocol require this behavior ? I read the rfc 793
    > > and couldnt find anything in there which imposes such a restriction
    > > on receiver.
    >
    >
    > One restriction is that the TCP receiver should never shrink the
    > advertised window.
    >

    the rfc says that such a behavior is "strongly discouraged" .

    i can not see the intution behind not allowing receiver
    to shrinks its windows. if the receiver shrinks its window
    after advertizing a large window, the worst case will be that
    (large window - shrinked window) amount of could be lost.
    this is not something disastrous as tcp can recover from this.
