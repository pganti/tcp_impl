This is the I-D for increasing TCP's initial window, which we discussed at
the DC and LA meetings. The plan is to now have it published as an
Experimental RFC. The tcp-impl revision of RFC 2001 will then standardize
the increase (modulo implementation experiences, of course).

So this is a Last Call for discussion of the I-D prior to submitting it to
the IESG.

                Vern

(The main technical change between versions -01 and -02 of the I-D concerns
 PMTU discovery: the I-D now simply notes that whether DF should be set on
 all of the initial packets, or just the first, is an open question.)

> A New Internet-Draft is available from the on-line Internet-Drafts directories.
>
>
> Title : Increasing TCP's Initial Window
> Author(s) : C. Partridge, S. Floyd, M. Allman
> Filename : draft-floyd-incr-init-win-02.txt
> Pages : 11
> Date : 21-Apr-98
>
> This document specifies an increase in the permitted initial window
> for TCP from one segment to roughly 4K bytes. This document
> discusses the advantages and disadvantages of such a change,
> outlining experimental results that indicate the costs and benefits
> of such a change to TCP.
