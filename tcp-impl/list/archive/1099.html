> It would also be wise to get your ISP to ensure that there is a full
> window*bandwidth of buffering on your port, which is surely far more than
> 4 segments. Having only 4 segments worth of buffering is likely to cause
> you significant grief regardless of proposed initial window change.

This doesn't work taken too far. With a 10 frame queue a bulk data connection
tends to capture the modem bandwidth to the total exclusion of other traffic.
In the Linux case many people drop the queue down quite low and you do get
much better link sharing from it

And yes it does ToS ordering and no ToS ordering isnt worth shit on the
internet nowdays. So much so that we had to add firewalling support for
ToS rewriting on the fly. While most stacks support IP_TOS options the
random clueless application code sold by a lot of people doesn't set it. I
guess Im thankful they've not discovered it yet or we'd have FTP servers
claiming everything is interactive/high reliability .
