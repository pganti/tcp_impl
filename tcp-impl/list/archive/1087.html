 sorry.]

> > > One connection with an N segment initial window is N times more
> > > aggressive in the first RTT.
>
> ...and N times more aggressive in all subsequent RTTs until the
> first packet loss is detected.

Right... I guess you could look at it that way. I was thinking of
the loss.

But, I would say that it is not N times more aggressive, but rather
the transfer is just shifted in time by a couple RTTs. In other
words, if you get 4 segments through the network initially, chances
are pretty good that you would have gotten 1 and 2 segments through
the network in the previous two RTTs. So, while we send N times
more segments each RTT than if we used an IW of 1 segment it is
fairly safe to assume that we would have made it to that window size
with an initial window of 1 segment (it just would have taken a
couple extra RTTs). (This is true for both multiple connections and
a larger initial window).
