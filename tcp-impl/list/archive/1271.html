About putting words in some RFC prohibiting bad ideas like TCP keepalives
repeated every 10 seconds--"let's not and say we did."

Such a prohibition would be both useless and wrong. It would be good to
urge restraint and say why, but outlawing any particular value, including
more than 1 keepalive per second, would not affect anyone who would do
such a thing, and should not. If you think about it a little, you can
contrive a case where 1 second keepalives are best engineering choice.
In the modern, you-use-it-you-pay-for-it, 10 Mbit/sec of out-of-window
probes are no better or worse than 10 Mbit/sec of UDP unicast audio or
pictures. If the customer wants to do it for good or stupid reasons, then
merely check their credit and sell 'em a big enough pipe. Yes, of course,
it would be dumb to use 1 sec. keepalives over a 1200 bps radio link; so
don't do that.

The IETF has no business outlawing stupidity that does not hurt bystanders.
Since the death of the NSFNet, bandwidth waste merely raises the bills
and trashes the performance of the wasteful, and hurts no one else.

The IETF already has more than enough RFCs that codify the obvious, make
stupidity illegal, support truth, justice, and the IETF way, and generally
demonstrate the author is a brilliant and valuable Contributor to The
Standards Process.
