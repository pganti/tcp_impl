> > "I'd like to jam the internet because my firewall is faulty". Thats the
> > same mentality as the linear backoff brigade.
>
> Pardon my flame, but, *what*?????
>
> You can argue that keepalives cause all sorts of problems, but a short
> packet every two hours on idle connections is going to jam the
> internet? Especially when most connections aren't set for keepalives?
> Get real. You'd need a probe of some sort anyway if you don't want to
> be obnoxious and throw off legitimately idle connections.

Im not arguing that der Mouse is doing the right thing in his circumstances.
But we can't make every random faulty piece of equipment an item to assess
when evaluating the need for keepalives.

In the firewall case a firewall itself can (and should have) been generating
its own state checks. It can send a spoofed keepalive transiting only
the local lan and eat what the keepalive reply.

The general case "my [link|firewall|proxy|...] doesnt work right therefore
Im going to cause a lot more traffic" in terms of an argument for the IETF
is not a reason to support that position within a standard. Nobody here
would support linear backoff right ? Instead technically superior but
harder to implement answers have been put forward.

How is der Mouse's firewall different except that he's the poor **** stuck
behind it.
