Hi,

I've got a couple of questions.

Tim Dorcey wrote:
> In case anyone else misunderstood my problem, the multiple slow rate
> connections discussed below involve different data to different
> destinations. It is not an attempt to bypass TCP flow control. The
> preference for UDP over TCP is to avoid continually opening and closing TCP
> connections for a single packet worth of data (since the number of
> "connections" is too large to maintain an open TCP connection to each one).
> What I also should have clarified is that the UDP traffic is single packet
> request-reply transactions, so the rate is limited by an effective "window
> size" of 1 packet, and actually much slower than (packet_size/RTT) because
> of the application needs.

    1) In a congestion situation, a TCP source's tx rate can fall well below
        (packet_size/RTT), being determined by some function of the
        current value of the source's timeout timer and the packet
        loss probability in the net. What do your clients do when
        when one of their requests gets lost?

    2) Are you talking about a LAN or WAN scenario? What would be
        a typical number of hops between your server and the clients.
