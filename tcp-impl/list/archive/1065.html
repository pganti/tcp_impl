> > 1) It will cause a burst of 4 segments to be sent by the sender
> > upon every ACK.

And, five segments per ACK during slow start (at least after the
window is larger than 4 segments).

> I fail to see how this would be any worse than alowing an IW of
> four segments. Surely if allowing an IW of four is acceptable,
> allowing an ACK for four segments should be acceptable too no? (Or
> did I mis-interpret the minutes of the last meeting?)

I think there IS a difference. The initial window burst happens
once per connection (OK, maybe more if it goes idle). If you use an
ACK interval of 4 segments the burst will happen much more often.
Not saying I think the burstiness that would be caused by an ACK
interval of 4 segments is acceptable or not, but I think it is
sufficiently different from the larger initial window proposal.

I agree with Mohit's nice analysis of the impact of the delayed ACK
timer when using an ACK interval of 4 segments. But, it should also
be noted that this also slows the growth of cwnd. If you ACK every
segment cwnd is doubled every RTT. Using an ACK interval of 2
segments halves the number of ACKs generated and therefore roughly
doubles the time it takes to open cwnd. Similarly, using an ACK
interval of 4 segments will require 4 times as much time to open
cwnd when compared to a ACKing every segment.
