In message <Pine.LNX.3.96.980423135355.30691A-100000@hill.ucr.edu>, Natchu Vish
nu Priya writes:

>So I drop around 12K of data which moved right throught the internet
>before I fall back to a 1 segment window size..
>and worse.. if all the connections do not have enough packets dropped.. I
>go one more round of this.. more packets dropped..
>
>I do understand that it falls back to 1 segment but at what cost.. and
>this is not an obscure example.. all modem links will have this problem..

Well, if the data gets all the way to your modem, then there's no congestion
and the economic cost of sending that data is zero.

The cost of dropping at your modem is negligible.

The performance results are just fine -- that's what Tim Shepard simulated.
You'll get the same behavior you had with 1 segment start.
