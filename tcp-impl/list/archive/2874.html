    >
    > Please, could someone tell me what happens at retransmission timers
    when
    > TCP sender enters in persist state? Are they stopped, reset or only
    > ignored? Is there a standard?
    >

    When the end point enters persist mode the retransmission counters
    continue to run.

    At the point when the retransmission timer expires, if the end point is
    still in persist mode than the packet will not be retransmitted. But the
    cwnd will be deflated because a retransmission timeout just occurred.

    If the retransmission timer had expired while the end point was in
    persist mode, then on exiting the persist mode, the packets are
    retransmitted. At the point when the packets are retransmitted, the new
    RTO clock starts ticking again. The new RTO is calculated for this first
    re-transmission as per the exponential back-off algorithm.
