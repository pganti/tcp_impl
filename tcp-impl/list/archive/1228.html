> In the dialup world, where the remote end disappears without notice
> quite frequently, not only is TCP keepalive a lifesaver but the appropriate
> interval is << 2 hours. Sorry, but that's just the way it is. If
> anybody has reason to disagree, I'd really appreciate hearing about it.

Take the number of dialup ports active on the internet. Multiply by 60%
to reflect the fact not all will be used. Multiply by the number of bytes
in a keepalive packet....

Now explain why you want to fill the internet with that magnitude of junk.
Keepalive is rarely needed. When there is active data flow the data flow
will discover the host loss, when there is not then most high level
protocols have their own timeouts.

Alan
