    > Subject: Tcp persist mode
    >
    > Hi,
    >
    > I'm wondering about TCP persist mode.
    >
    > As you know, if receiver sends ACK with 0 window size, sender stops
    > sending new data.
    >
    > Consider this scenario:
    >
    > Suppose that sender transmitted segments [1] [2] [3] [4] [5] [6].
    Than,
    > receiver sends ack for segment [2] and shrinks window to zero.
    >
    > when sender receives it, enters persist mode and stops sending new
    data.
    >
    > After, receiver sends ack for segment [4] and reopens window.
    >
    > My questions are:
    >
    >
    >
    > 1) what happens at segment [5] and [6] ? Are they retransmitted by
    sender?
    > If they are retransmitted, when sender retransmits them?

    Yes sender should retransmit them after the retransmission timeout,
    provided by then the sender has come out of the persist mode.

    > Must the sender
    > wait receiver to reopen window or can retransmits them when it is in
    > persist state?
    >

    No retransmissions are allowed in the persist state.

    >
    > 2) Could sender shrinks its congestion window, in this scenario?
    >
    >

    The sender can choose to always shrink congestion window on
    retransmission. It may choose to ignore the fact that the retransmission
    occurred because of the receiver shrunk the window.

    > Thanks in advance.
    > P.
