>> like have an initial idle time of five minutes, and if the peer
>> responds to that keepalive, goose the idle time up to two hours or
>> so for all future keepalives - the idea being that basically all
>> connections are either very transient (for which even five minutes
>> is perhaps a bit long) or reasonably long-lived (the sort of thing
>> for which the two-hour default was designed).
> But isn't such exponential backing off still in NetBSD, derived from
> 1986's 4.3BSD?

Exponential backoff? No, not at all. Using the defaults (which as I
read tcp_timer.h are 2 hours idle, 8 probes, with 75 seconds between
probes), if the last packet on the connection was at 10:00:00 and the
peer goes away, keepalive probes will take place at 12:00:00, 12:01:15,
12:02:30, 12:03:45, 12:05:00, 12:06:15, 12:07:30, and 12:08:45; if
nothing is still received at 12:10:00, the connection will be
considered broken. (I may be off-by-one; that might describe a KEEPCNT
of 9, rather than 8.)

Are you thinking of transmission backoff when there *is* data to send,
perhaps? Remember, these are keepalive timers, not retransmission
timers or zero-window persist timers.

> Or do you mean to do a steeper ramp than the 4.*BSD backoff from the
> initial probe interval?

What I meant was not a ramp at all, but a step. Something like sending
keepalive probes per the above algorithm with an initial idle time of
something like 5 minutes. If peer doesn't respond, of course,
connection is broken. If peer does respond, the connection is marked
such that in the future, the connection has to idle two hours (or
whatever) before provoking keepalives.

Thus, in the absence of other traffic and in the absence of lost
packets, you'd see something like

10:00:00.000 Connection goes idle
10:05:00.000 Keepalive probe sent
10:05:00.125 ACK received
12:05:00.125 Keepalive probe sent
12:05:00.250 ACK received
14:05:00.250 Keepalive probe sent
14:05:00.375 ACK received

Or maybe with some real traffic thrown in,

10:00:00.000 Connection goes idle
10:05:00.000 Keepalive probe sent
10:05:00.125 ACK received
12:05:00.125 Keepalive probe sent
12:05:00.250 ACK received
13:27:08.440 Real traffic begins
13:28:44.106 Real traffic stops, connection goes idle
15:28:44.106 Keepalive probe sent
15:28:44.241 ACK received
17:28:44.241 Keepalive probe sent
17:28:44.396 ACK received

I'd still prefer per-connection values, though. :-)
