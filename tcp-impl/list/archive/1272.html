Vernon,

In some sense, "let's not and say we did" is exactly what RFC 1122 did
already, so your empassioned statement does not add anything new to
this issue. Rest assured that there are people in the Internet
community, equally competant and well-intentioned with yourself, who do
not accept all of what you wrote. There are good arguments on both
sides of this issue. However, this does not seem to be the time or
place to go over the intellectual arguments again; I am not aware of
any intent to rewrite this section of RFC 1122.

Indeed, I believe that the compromise taken by RFC-1122 is probably the
right one: be extremely conservative by default, but allow software
engineers to be less conservative with keep-alives when necessary and
when fully knowledgable about the possible bad consequences.

Bob Braden
