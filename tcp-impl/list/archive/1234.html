(Goodness, my note seems to have provoked more traffic on this list in
the last week than I saw on it in the previous month! :-)

> I've seen two major classes of customers that want keepalives and
> want them to take place at frequent intervals: [...]

I don't know about either of those; all I know is the reason *I* wanted
keepalives. (Actually, what I want is the idle time and the probe
interval to be short, but the number of probes before the connection is
killed high, so that keepalives are sent soon and relatively
frequently, but connections don't die on short network outages.)

So what is that reason? Connections through a stateful firewall that
drops state if it sees no traffic for a relatively short period. (I
think the firewall in question has its timeout set to 5 minutes.) I
used to lose connecctions regularly until I realized what was going on
and hacked run-time tuning for those keepalive values into the kernel I
was using.

Yes, I would much prefer to get rid of the firewall. Unfortunately, it
ain't gonna go away; to get it out of my hair would mean getting rid of
that job.
