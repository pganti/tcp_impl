On Wed, 22 Apr 1998, Vern Paxson wrote:

> > This document specifies an increase in the permitted initial window
> > for TCP from one segment to roughly 4K bytes. This document
> > discusses the advantages and disadvantages of such a change,
> > outlining experimental results that indicate the costs and benefits
> > of such a change to TCP.

For a particular connection assuming a rtt of 100ms means that a TCP will
start out with a rate of 4K/100ms = 40 KBps.

For smaller rtts it is even greater. Isnt this greater than the bandwidth
available for each connection on most links during congestion.

What this will mean is that when the average rate available for each
connection is low the rtt will build up (to 4K/rate) which will be pretty
high.. or there will considerable packet loss due to buffer overflows.

In conditions where the bandwidth available for each connection is high,
this seems to be a good idea (reduces latency), but during congestion
might be a very bad idea.

Do the simulation results contradict this.

vishnu
