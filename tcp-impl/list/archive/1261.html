Suppose I have a firewall that doesnt do state checks by spoofing
keepalives internally. Now I sell it to 3ciscoscendnet and their bright
marketing people provide a nice windows configuration disk that sets the
40,000 corporate windows PC's to use 5 minute keepalives. Now sell those
to every major corporation.

Fixing the firewall would have been the right thing to do - obviously. And
furthermore as my initial comment showed you can fix that without changes
to the client PC's by spoofing keepalives and stealing the ack in the firewall
itself [sure der mouse can't in this case but on that issue he is [pardon the
insult] a user.

This is completely seperate to the issue of "are keepalives good or bad"
"Is 2hrs now overlong as a limit" "should we standardise knobs for setting
it". All Im pointing out is that said misimplemented firewall is not relevant
to the discussion. It is broken. Vern's http server resource point is relevant
as are his comments on packet size. And those are worth listening to.

Now I'll buy a lot of Vern's arguments for shorter keepalives due to resource
issues and the fact a protocol keepalive is no less resource using.
