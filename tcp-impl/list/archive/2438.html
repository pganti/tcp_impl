    I exchanged some mail with the guy who wrote that original note (saying
    that Linux's RFC 1323 implementation was adding delay). He said the issue
    as he recalled it was when a Linux box was acting as a router, and slowing
    down when handling RFC 1323 timestamps sent with every packet. I'm not
    sure why that would matter since from the router's perspective the
    timestamps are just payload. Anyway, I just ran a few ttcp tests between
    two hosts connected by switched 100bt through a router (all 3 machines
    running RedHat 7) and I got 85-90 Mbps...
